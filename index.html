Ken Miles

The speed machine genius

Ken Miles (left) at the Le Mans of '66 with Carol Shelby

Here's the timeline of Ken Miles' life

  • 1918 - Born in The Royal Town of Sutton Coldfield
  • 1933 - Left school at 15 to be an apperentice at Wolseley Motors. Raced motorcycles before serving the British Territorial Army in the World War II
  • 1942 - Became the staff sergeant
  • 1944 - Stationed in a tank unit in the Normandy landings. Went and raced against Buggatti, Alfa Roemeos and Alvises with the Vintage Sports Car Club after the war. He then joined Ford V8 Frazer-Nash
  • 1952 - Moved to the US as a service manager for Gough Industries, the Souther California MG distributer
  • 1953 - Won 14 straight victories in SCCA racing in his own design and construction of MG
  • 1955 - Designed, constructed and campaigned a second unique MG components known as the "Flying Shingle". He was then qualified in the Palm Spring as his fenders were too wide.
  • 1956 - Raced John von Neumann's Porche 550 Spyder at most of the Cal Club and SCCA events
  • 1957 - In co-operation with Otto Zipper, Miles engineered the installation of Porche 550s engine and transmssion in a 1956 Cooper chasis and body. It was successful in the West Coast and was known as "the Pooper", with Miles dominating the F Modified class of SCCA in the 1957 & 1958 seasons.
  • 1963 - Joined Shelby/Cobra race team in early 60s and became the chief test driver of Shelby-American.
  • 1964 - Participated Lotus 23 in the 1964 Player's 200 at Mosport as a member of the AC-Cobra Ford Team
  • 1965 - Share Ford GT Mk.II with Bruce McLaren at the 24 Hours of Le Mans but retied due to gearbox issue. He later race again with Bruce McLaren and finished 2nd at the 12 Hours of Sebring
  • 1966 - Won the 24 Hours of Daytona, sharing the Ford GT Mk.II with Lloyd Ruby. Miles was leading the 1966 24 Hours of Le Mans, but Ford executive Leo Beebe, deised a publicity photo and instructed three of their cars crossing the finish line together. The next car from Ford driven by Bruce McLaren and Chris Amon, and cruised the line together. McLaren crossed the finish line just ahead and declared winner. Miles was then denied the unique achievement of winning Sebring, Daytona, and Le Mans in the same year.
  • 1966 - dies at the age of 48 due to fatal accident while testing the Ford J-Car
